What are the impacts of climate change?

by Wesley Castro

Major threats to society and health

Affects already being seen due to climate change include changes in our food and water sources, the air we breathe, the weather we experience currently and in future.

The rising increase of greenhouse gasses in the atmosphere results in disturbance of temperature, precipitation, frequency and intensity of extreme weather events along with rising sea levels.

Extreme temperature changes have been correlated to increased levels of illness and death by compromising the bodyâ€™s ability to regulate its temperature or by inducing direct or indirect health complications. Modified weather patterns have influenced the levels and location of outdoor air pollutants such as ground-level ozone and fine particulate matter making air quality poor. Whether outdoors or indoors changes in air quality can negatively affect the human respiratory and cardiovascular systems.

How can we make a change?

Reversing the affects of climate change is easily reachable as there are many ways society can have a positive impact on the ecosystem. Breakthrough technology with sustainable energy has made it even easier to reduce pollution due to natural gasses. Protecting trees from being cutdown and preservation of water along with electrical useage also reduces the negative affects on the earth. Sustainability is easier than ever with a quick search on google of how you can make a difference.
